Welcome to IRCN
Armenia

A community based non-profit press organization

IRCNA is built upon a simple belief: people living the story should be able to help tell it. i'm Mikael, the founder and journalist. I created IRCNA to filla gap - independent, English-language reporting and journalism from Armenia that doesn't just talk to you, but listens first. My reporting covers politics, breaking events, festivals and the everyday realities that don't always make headlines. IRCNA is funded entirely by it's community, not by political parties nor corporate owners. If you value journalismrooted in place and accountable to its audience, you're in the right place.

Our Mission

Unbiased Reporting

IRCNA exists to bring you facts, not partisan spin. Every story is reported from the ground, verified against multiple sources, and told without fear or favor. As a nonprofit, nonpartisan initiative, we answer only to our community - not political parties, oligarchs, or foreign agendas. Our loyalty is to accuracy, context and the truth, giving you the information you need to understand Armenia on it's own terms.

Community Connection

Our journalism begins by listening. IRCNA's "community networking" model means your questions, tips and on-the-ground perspectives directly shape what we cover. Through tip lines, conversations, and open calls, we close the gap between journalist and audience. This isn't a one-way broadcast; it's a relationship built on mutual trust. When you send us a lead, you're not just a source - you're a partner in the reporting process.

Giving Armenians a Voice

Too often stories about Armenia are filtered through outside voices or loud partisan megaphones. IRCNA flips that script. We amplify the voices of ordinary people - residents of Yerevan neighborhoods, villagers in the regions, diaspora members with stake in the homeland - and ensure thier experiences, struggles, and hopes are part of the public record. In english, we make sure these perspectives reach a wider world that rarely hears them directly.

Supporting Independent Journalism

Independent reporting in Armenia can't survive on good intentions alone. IRCNA is built to be community-funded: powered by donations, memberships, and grants rather than advertising or state money. By building an audience that directly supports our work, we safeguard editorial freedom. When you contribute, you're not just sustaining one journalist - you're helping prove that sustainable, trustworthy, English-language journalism from Armenia is possible.
